Elements of Good Web Design

The core of any website is, of course, the content, whether it's stories, important information, things for sale... Your website is only as good as the content it contains. But websites are a visual medium, and the way in which you present information is crucial. Great web design should be functional, easy to use, and beautiful.

🌌

Space

Use white space to relax the viewer and focus the eye to important content.

⌨

Text

Use a large, legible font. Use size and weight to differentiate between levels of headers/body. Above all, use text sparingly.

🖍

Colors

Pick a color scheme with 1-4 colors. Use small pops of color to focus attention. Ensure high contrast between text and background-colors.

🖼️

Images

A picture is worth a thousand words, particularly when designing websites. Display images in consistent sizes, styles, and locations on the page.

Case Studies: Good Design

New York Public LibraryClick for more

nypl.org

New York Public Libraryclose

The NYPL website is both beautiful and highly functional. It makes excellent use of color to delineate different sections and fantastic use of images. The dominant red and black color scheme is bold, yet its restrained application prevents the page from becomming oppressive. The navbar makes the site easy to navigate. One con is the changing color scheme on other pages of the site, which is slightly jarring.

Blue ApronClick for more

blueapron.com

Blue Apronclose

This website contrasts cute cartoon graphics with high-quality photographs to create an aesthetic that is both sophisticated and warm. The 3-column meal layout conserves space, appeals visually, and places focus on most important thing: food. One drawback is the video background on the landing page, which is distracting.


Case Studies: Bad Design

Evan's Regents Chemistry CornerClick for more

evanschemistrycorner.com

Evan's Regents Chemistry Cornerclose

Evan's Chemistry Corner is a wonderful website full of fantastic resources for high school chemistry teachers. Unfortunately, the design of the site undercuts its value. The page layout is cramped. The graphics are corny. The navigation links on the side are small and difficult to read due to low contrast between the font color and the unnecessarily-ombré background. On pages with more text, the text is squashed together into a narrow vertical column without sufficient spacing between lines, making the page both hard to read and unnecessarily long. This website has some fantastic content, and it's a shame the design doesn't reflect that.

SkedulaClick for more

skedula.com

Skedulaclose

This website takes the cake for terrible design. The colors are inconsistent and the black sidebar is jarring. There are three different navegation bars-- which should you use?? The icons are cramped instead of being distributed evenly across the screen. The font is small and difficult to read. The sheer amount of graphics and information makes it impossible to discern what is important. Fun fact: the styling of the publicly-accessible page is completely unrelated to the main site. The only way to experience the true horror of this monstrosity is to pay for a subscription.
